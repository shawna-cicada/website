# Publishing on cicadaagility.com — Editor's Guide

This guide is for you if you write and publish articles and videos on the
Cicada Agility website. You do not need any technical knowledge — if a step
ever feels like it requires some, that's a problem with the tool, not with
you. Tell whoever maintains the site.

> **Where to go:** the editorial dashboard lives at
> **cicadaagility.com/admin**. Enter the team password first (ask
> Shawna — it is shared privately, never written down here), then sign
> in with your usual account.

---

## What you see after signing in

You land on the **Cicada Agility dashboard** — not a technical content
list. It shows big action buttons and your recent work.

*[Screenshot: dashboard with the eight action cards and recent-content lists]*

The actions:

- **Create a New Article** — start a fresh article from a template.
- **Add a Video** — share a video or recorded conversation.
- **Edit Existing Content** — find and update anything already written.
- **Schedule a Post** — open a draft, then use its Schedule button.
- **Update Homepage Content** — change what the homepage features.
- **Manage Assessments** — edit assessment cards and their links.
- **Manage Client Logos** — add logos and confirm permissions.
- **Preview the Website** — open the live site in a new tab.

Below the actions: **Recent drafts**, **Scheduled posts**, **Recently
published**, and **Needs attention** (pieces missing something required).
Each shows when it was last edited and its status badge.

### Status badges

| Badge | Means |
| --- | --- |
| **Draft** | Work in progress. Only you and other editors can see it. |
| **Needs Review** | Ready for a second pair of eyes. Use **Send for Review** in the actions menu (the ⋮ next to Publish) — it moves the piece to the Waiting-for-review list without publishing anything. The same menu offers **Back to Draft** to reverse it. |
| **Scheduled** | Will go live automatically at the chosen time. |
| **Published** | Live on the website. |
| **Archived** | Off the website, kept safely — restorable any time. |

## Writing an article, start to finish

### 1. Choose what you're making

Click **Create a New Article** (or **Add a Video**). You'll pick from
visual templates:

- **Leadership Insight** — a pattern you keep seeing in leadership teams.
- **Founder Lesson** — a growth-stage challenge founders face.
- **Practical Guide** — step-by-step help for one improvement.
- **Video Post** — a video with context and takeaways.
- **Client or Case Insight** — a real situation and its lessons.

*[Screenshot: template picker]*

### 2. Write

The editor opens with only the essentials:

- **What is the title?** — your headline. The page's web address is
  created from it automatically; you never have to think about it.
- **How would you summarize this?** — one or two sentences.
- **Who wrote this?** — pick the author.
- **What image should appear at the top?** — upload and describe it in a
  few words (that description is read aloud for people using screen
  readers).
- **The piece itself** — write like in a document: headings, lists,
  quotes, links, images. For videos: paste the video link instead.
- **Which category fits best?**

Templates pre-fill the piece with an outline. Lines starting with **✎**
are writing prompts — replace them with your own words. (The system will
politely refuse to publish while any ✎ line remains, so they can never
sneak onto the website.)

**Your work saves automatically** as you type, and every version is kept —
you can always go back (see "Fixing mistakes" below).

*[Screenshot: the editing screen with the essentials fields]*

There's an **Advanced settings** tab you can ignore completely — search
titles, descriptions, and the page address are filled in automatically.

### 3. Preview it

Click **Preview** in the actions menu. The piece opens exactly as readers
will see it. Check it on your phone too — the preview link works there.

*[Screenshot: desktop preview and mobile preview side by side]*

### 4. Prepare the LinkedIn post

In the actions menu, click **Copy LinkedIn Post**. The system writes a
suggested post from your title and summary, including the article's link,
copies it to your clipboard, and marks it "Ready to post".

You can edit the suggestion first in the **Promotion** tab. After you post
it on the Cicada Agility LinkedIn page, paste the LinkedIn post's URL into
the Promotion tab and set the status to **Posted**.

*[Screenshot: Promotion tab with generated post text]*

### 5. Publish

Three choices in the actions menu:

- **Save Draft** — happens automatically anyway; just close when done.
- **Schedule** — pick a future date and time; it goes live by itself.
- **Publish Now** — live immediately.

Before publishing, the system runs a friendly checklist (title, summary,
image and its description, author, category, content, no leftover ✎
prompts). If something's missing, it tells you exactly what and how to fix
it — in plain words. Nothing is published until the critical items pass.

*[Screenshot: pre-publish checklist dialog]*

## Finding and editing something later

From the dashboard, **Edit Existing Content**, or use the lists on the
dashboard itself. Content is grouped by status (Drafts, Scheduled,
Published, Archived) — no searching through technical menus.

## Taking something off the website

- **Unpublish** — takes it off the site and keeps it as a draft.
- **Archive** — tucks it away in the Archived list. The system always
  asks before archiving, and **nothing is ever deleted** — archived pieces
  can be restored any time.

## Fixing mistakes

Every change is kept in the piece's history (the clock icon in the top
right of the editor). Open it, pick an earlier version, and choose
**Restore Previous Version** — it comes back as a draft for you to review
and re-publish.
